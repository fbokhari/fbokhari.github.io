General Background

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Economics and the Centre for Competition Policy (CCP) at the University of East Anglia (UEA).   I am an empirical economist and most of my research uses applied microeconimic theory and idustrial organization (IO) and applies it to health care.  I have a strong policy focus.

I am also a co-editor at Economic Inquiry.

My main research interests include competition policy and antitrust with a special interest in health care, the hospital sector, and pharmaceuticals.  I have worked on the valuation of new pharmaceutical products used in the treatment of ADHD, including generics and second-generation drugs (`me-too' drugs) as well as firm strategies for growth and entry deterrence (presentation proliferation, pay-for-delay, product hopping). Currently, I am also working on regulations surrounding the consumption of alcohol and the use of antibiotic drugs.

I also work in the area of outcomes research and research methods in health care. 

I got my Ph.D. in Public Policy and Management from the Heinz School at Carnegie Mellon University, USA. Before joining UEA, I was a Lecturer (Assistant prof) at King’s College London (Department of Primary Care and Public Health Sciences) and before that an Assistant Professor at Florida State University (Department of Economics). I also did a two-year post-doctoral stint at the University of California, Berkeley (School of Public Health).
